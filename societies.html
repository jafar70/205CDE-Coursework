Societies

Societies at Coventry

Here at Coventry we offer a broad range of societies! From course-based groups, to those based around religious, political or ethical sensibilities. Many of our societies are based on common interests, such as the popular Adventure Society, or the newly formed SoberSquad. In 2014 we became the first students' union to offer free societies - an offer that has since been replicated at other institutions. The removal of membership fees has seen participation in our societies increase substantially, and the number of fun and varied activities has soared.

Societies regularly run a number of social events, meetings and trips throughout the year – whether it’s a trip into town, up to Edinburgh, down to London to see a show or a night at the Students’ Union – there’s something for everyone. Joining a society is also an exciting opportunity to learn something new, like salsa or surfing; visit somewhere you’ve never been before, or further develop a passion or skill you already have! To see a full list of the societies currently on offer have a look at our Societies A-Z List or check out our Societies Handbook below, and if you can’t find the society you are looking for, then why not start your own?! It’s simple – check out our 5 simple steps to starting a new society to find out how. As your Students' Union we are here to make sure that you have the best time whilst at University, so if there is a society that we don't currently offer we can help you set up your own! The Most Popular Societies

AFRICAN CARIBBEAN SOCIETY

Aims and Objectives
1) Bridging the gap between international students and home students

2) Educating other cultures about African and Caribbean traditions, customs and culture

3) Providing opportunities for neighbouring universities to unite for workshops and events

4) Entertaining and enlightening students on African and Caribbean culture

5) Empowering and enlightening with positive prospects for the future e.g. work experience and placements

Membership Fee
Membership for the society is £5.00 for the whole academic year.

LABOUR

Aims and Objectives
1) To get more young people interested and involved in how we are governed.

2) To give people with like minded political beliefs and values a change to socialise.

3) To allow for more networking.

4) To give people a chance to reflect upon current issues around politics.

5) An opportunity to socialise and meet people from all works of life.

Membership Fee
Membership for the society is £4.00 for the whole academic year.

GAMING SOCIETY

Aims and Objectives for the Gaming Society
1) To create a place for people who share an interest in games and gaming to meet, make friends and above all have fun.

2) To encourage members to experience various different forms of gaming.

3) To provide a place where people can play games

4) To meet at least once a week

5) To provide new games for people to play.

Membership Fee
Membership for the society is £5 for the whole academic year.
